Who We Are

A Voluntary Organisation
Built on Faith & Service

The name FATIMA comes from Portugal where the Virgin Mary appeared to three children in 1917, emphasising the power of prayer. This inspired our founder to create a centre dedicated to prayer and community transformation.

Located in Kidodo Cell, Central Division, Kasese Municipality, Uganda, we serve as a "Home for All" — regardless of appearance, culture, tribe, gender, age, religion or education level.

Our Vision

Spiritual empowerment for orphans, PWDs, elders and widows.

Our Mission

Building a strong praying working team to pool resources.

Core Values

Inclusivity, compassion, faith, transparency, and service.

Our Reach

Serving Kasese District and beyond with 300+ members.

"A father to over 250 children, regardless of tribe, faith or background."

Our Founder

Alfred Bwambale

Founder & Spiritual Leader — Omuzana

Alfred Bwambale, known as Omuzana, was born on February 11, 1992 in Kaghema, Kyarumba sub-county. A devout Catholic, he was born with a special gift from God — the gift of prayer and healing that draws people from across Uganda and beyond.

Alfred owns the FAPCAN church where he prays for people of different religions. Beyond being a prayer warrior, he is a father to over 250 children — providing food, shelter, education, skills, and inspiration since 2007.
