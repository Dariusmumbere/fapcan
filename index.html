About FAPCAN

Fatima Praying Center for All Nations - A voluntary non-government organization founded on May 25, 2012

Our Vision & Mission

The name FATIMA comes from Portugal where Virgin Mary appeared to three children in 1917, emphasizing the power of prayer. This inspired our founder to create a center dedicated to prayer and community transformation.

VISION: To create an association reputed for spiritual empowerment for orphans, PWDs, elders, widows, women and their families through life transformation, education, accommodation and income generation projects.

MISSION: To build a strong praying working team with one voice, to pool ideas and resources together for enhancement of orphans, PWDs, elders, widows, women emancipation, social-economic and spiritual development.

Located in Kidodo Cell, Central division, Kasese Municipality, Kasese District, Uganda, we serve as a "HOME FOR ALL" regardless of appearance, culture, tribe, gender, age, religion or education level.

Our Founder

Alfred Bwambale (Omuzana) - A man with a special gift from God

Alfred Bwambale

Founder & Spiritual Leader

Alfred Bwambale, also known as Omuzana, was born on February 11, 1992, in Kaghema - Kyarumba sub-county. He is the 5th child of Baluku Wilson Serebethe and Bujune Christine, a Mukonzo by tribe and Ugandan by nationality.

Alfred is a married man with 4 children and a devout Catholic born with a special gift from God. He owns the FAPCAN church where he prays for people of different religions from Uganda and beyond.

Beyond being a prayer warrior, Alfred is a father to over 250 children that he cares for regardless of their culture, tribe, gender, age, religion, or appearance. He has provided food, shelter, education, skills and inspiration to countless people since 2007.

Alfred is also a creative talent - a film actor and producer who owns Save Souls Film Industry with the purpose of saving people's souls from hell, challenging social living, showcasing stories, cultural heritage, and spreading the gospel.
